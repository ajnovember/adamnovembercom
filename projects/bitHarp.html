bitHarp
bitHarp is a touch sensing MIDI harp that I made in Spring 2014 for a multimedia art class at NYU. The harp is about 2 feet tall with 18 strings. I used a Teensy microcontroller and program them to use capacitive sensing, which allows the microcontroller to sense human touch through its pins. The pins are all soldered to copper strings. When a touch is sensed on a string, the Teensy sends a MIDI note via USB. I wrote a Max Msp program that synthesizes the sounds and controls the scale and key that the harp plays in, but it can also be used as a plug and play USB MIDI controller. Bit crushing on the harp is controlled via a generic Line 6 expression pedal. bitHarp won 2nd place in the 2014 NYU Music Technology Design Competition.
