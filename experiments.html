experiments
MIDI pendulum
Here, a reed (magnet) switch senses a swinging magnet and sends impulses to a Teensy microcontroller. The Teensy then sends MIDI messages via USB to any DAW, in this case Ableton Live.
bitHarp 2.0
This is a larger, 6 foot, 28 string version of my bitHarp that I created specifically to bring to the STEAM:Works festival in May 2015.
rubber band pitch bend
These are experiments I did with conductive rubber bands. I discovered that these rubber bands change their internal resistance when streched, so I could measure this change and convert it to a pitch bend amount. I am using capacitive sensing to read rubber bands.
physical groove pizza
A prototype of a physical Groove Pizza, which uses metal washers to complete circuits and register rhythms on the face of the pizza
photo theremin
A simple instrument composed of a photo resistor, being read by a Teensy microcontroller, sending sound to a speaker. The Teensy is programmed such that the more light the photoresistor senses, the higher the pitch produced.
